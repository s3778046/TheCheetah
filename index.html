Threats

Some of the most significant threats to the cheatah include; Hunting and poaching, farmers protecting livestock, climate change, the destruction of habitat, low rate of reproductive success compared to other mammals.

Education

Education is vital to the survival of the cheetah, research shows that puplic education will help ensure the survival of the cheetah into the future.

Livelihood Program

A major key to ensuring the survival of the cheetah into the future is to secure the livelihood of people who share habitat with the species. People who develop livelihood skills without the need to exploit the cheetah for income will have a major impact on the survival of the species.

Cheetah sustainability program

The cheetah sustainability program is a breeding program to support conservation of the cheetah and ensure the survival of the species. Litters of cheetahs are being breed in large breeding centers to help the repopulation of the species.

Livestock guarding dogs

The livestock guarding dog program is a non lethal predator control method that helps farmers and wildlife co-exist peacefully. The guard dogs reduce the cheetahs ability to get close to farm animals reducing the need for farmers to kill cheetahs to protect there livestock.
